Tara Gum

Tara gum is a natural additive, obtained by grinding the endosperm of the seeds of Caesalpinia spinosa, of the Leguminosae family. Tara gum, also called Peruvian carob, is a white to yellowish powder which is soluble in hot water and partially soluble in cold water.

Product description

The Tara seeds, extracted from the pods by threshing, are sieved to remove impurities and then roasted to break the external hull, which is tough and hard, and to eliminate the germ and husk. Tara gum in the form of a split is obtained then milled with deionised water and sieved to produce commercial Tara gum in powder form. The Tara gum extracted from the endosperm is a white to yellowish, odourless powder, which is soluble in hot water and partially soluble in cold water but not in ethanol.

Usage

Tara gum is predominantly used as a replacement for locust bean gum and guar gum. Aglumix®01 is used in a wide range of food applications such as baked goods, dairy products, frozen desserts, water jellies, fruit preparations, meat based products, sauces and dressings.
